Senso Ji Temple

Senso-ji Temple, located in Asakusa, Tokyo, is the city's oldest and a significant Buddhist temple dedicated to Kannon, the goddess of mercy.

The iconic Thunder Gate, Nakamise-dori shopping street, and the main hall with its Five-Storied Pagoda make Senso-ji a cultural and historical symbol of Tokyo. It attracts millions of visitors annually, offering a glimpse into Japan's rich traditions and religious practices.

Kyoto City view

Kyoto boasts a captivating cityscape blending modernity with historical charm. From vantage points like Kyoto Tower, you can witness a panorama that includes traditional temples, historic structures, and the scenic Higashiyama Mountains.

The juxtaposition of ancient and contemporary elements defines Kyoto's unique and picturesque urban landscape.

Cherry Blossoms

Kyoto's famed cherry blossom season, or "Sakura," paints the city in hues of pink and white from late March to early April. Prime spots like Maruyama Park, the Philosopher's Path, and Kiyomizu-dera offer enchanting views.

Locals and visitors partake in traditional activities like hanami picnics, creating a magical and ephemeral atmosphere in Kyoto.
